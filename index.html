Digital Collection

The following is a summary of various works developed that were required to support the business I was employed at, to expand my knowledge in the technical documentation field and prepare for the coming changes around digital transformation.
Read the summaries and then click on the appropriate links for examples of the work developed.

Digitalise Standards

Utilising the framework of a working Government Standard website, I transitioned the current standards required for Defence publications from PDFs to basic websites. This was to provide and electronic resource that was easier to navigate. They were not made to be commercialised.

Standards Quick Reference

To help users find information in a static PDF quicker, an infographic was developed for each standard to provide quick reference to the location of relevant chapter or section. If there was an overall grouping of topics provided then that was used as the framework for the relevant content and to align with the publication.

Introductory Learning Material

Developed introductory learning packages for various elements of Defence standards and specifications. With the move towards utilising a more visual warning nomenclature the more recent Australian Defence standards have aligned more with the globally harmonized system of safety icons.Graphics and singal words have been developed to convey a clearer understanding of the hazard posed.

Blog Articles

Developed a series of blog articles for my current company that provided research and information on topics within technical documentation, but also provided an look at existing subjects and explained them in the context of how they affected business.

Techincal Writing Career Assistance Guide

After experiencing many years of not really being able to find a guide that explains what is needed to be a technical writer/author. There was a time in the past when this role was well staffed and tertiary coursed. However with changing technology and expectations of other roles to do more documentation, techincal writers have been relagated to the eidtorial clean up aspects of document development. I am hoping this guide will promote a better understanding of what technical writers can actually do, and how they are crucial to the transition from paper based manuals to interactive digital publications

Legacy Defence Publications

This is an example of a simple PDF publication being hosted on a server. The publication can be flipped like a book and have simple navigation options externally to preliminary pages, table of contents, sections/chapters and the list of abbreviations. The intention was to provide some functionality to existing legacy publications that would not be worth the cost of transitioning to an S1000D IETP or similar. The additional artwork cover was to provide a sense of ownership for the particular service and contact information that would be useful.

About Me

Key Experience

  • A capable and experienced Technical Author with a strong background and skill sets in Project Management,electronics,
    mechanical technologies and Integrated Logistic Support (ILS).
  • Adaptable, excellent communicator and team player, with twenty-five years of experience in both the Australian Defence Force and private industry.
  • Recently completed developing a suite of User Handbooks for the LAND2110 CBRND Project in accordance with DEF(AUST)5629B. Managing a small team of writers.
  • Whilst at BAE Systems, over a three-year period, completed the Heating, Ventilation and Air Conditioning System Manual on the new Amphibious Landing Helicopter Deck (LHD) for the Royal Australian Navy. Created with Adobe FrameMaker 10 using a structured authoring environment.
  • 11 years of experience developing military and civilian documentation including Electrical and Engineering Maintenance Instructions (EMEI), User Handbooks, Repair Parts Scales, Repair Parts Item Lists, Intermediate Level Maintenance Procedures, Maintenance and Operator Manuals for Melbourne Water and Technicians Maintenance Manuals for the Holden motor company.
  • Project management, development and delivery of technical documentation for the JP2059 Phase 2A Centrifugal Blower Assembly Project. Provided support in maintaining the project timelines, updating issue and risk logs and liaising on behalf of the prime contractor.
  • Two years of experience developing and creating tender responses for Defence projects including JP126, JP2095 and Land 121 Phase 3A under the ASDEFCON suite of tendering and contracting templates. Providing support and liaising with key prime contractor stakeholders and the Commonwealth to ensure valid tender responses.
  • Earlier documentation experience involved various diverse projects including work on the Land 121 Trailer tender bid for Haulmark Trailers, development of the tender bid for the JP126 Joint Distribution Theatre Forklift Project and the Aviation Fire Truck tender. There was also work on Melbourne Water's 25W Lagoon Project at Werribee Treatment Plant, the Eastlink Project and Technicians Maintenance Manuals for various Holden motor vehicles.
  • Six years in the Air Force as an Avionics Technician streaming as a Measuring and Flight Systems Specialist. The development and subsequent introduction of an intermediate level, portable test set for the F-111G Inertial Navigation Units. During the handover of the equipment to the Civilian Support Program (CSP), nominated as the Subject Matter Expert and required to provide On-job Training to the incoming workforce.
